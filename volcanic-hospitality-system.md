## 00 · The Shift

Estancia Cafetera is not a coffee brand.
Estancia Cafetera is not a boutique hotel project.
Estancia Cafetera is not a branding case study.

Those are outputs.

**Estancia Cafetera is a Volcanic Hospitality System** — a hospitality sanctuary where coffee becomes a daily ritual connecting guests to the volcanic landscape, craftsmanship, and slower rhythms of Tepoztlán.

> Coffee is not the product. **Presence is the product.**

---

## 01 · Purpose

Modern life fragments attention.

Estancia exists to create small moments of reconnection — through hospitality, coffee, nature, and ritual.

Every design decision answers a single question:
**How do we help people slow down and reconnect?**

---

## 02 · The Four Experience Pillars

### Ritual
Coffee is treated like a ceremony. Not caffeine. Not productivity. A ritual.

### Earth
Everything feels rooted in place. Volcanic stone. Clay. Wood. Local vegetation. Handmade materials.

### Hospitality
Guests feel hosted. Not processed.

### Craftsmanship
Nothing feels mass-produced. Everything feels considered.

---

## 03 · Visual Direction — The 40/30/20/10 Formula

Stop thinking *coffee brand.*
Start thinking *volcanic hospitality system.*

Most coffee brands lead with product. **Estancia inverts the ratio:**

```
40% — ARCHITECTURE
       Stone walls. Terracotta arches. Courtyards. Shadows.
       Pathways. Fireplaces. SPACES are the strongest images.

30% — RITUAL
       Hands. Coffee preparation. Ceramics. Textures.
       Morning light. The act of slowing down.

20% — LANDSCAPE
       Tepoztlán mountains. Volcanic formations.
       Mist. Vegetation. Place as identity.

10% — PRODUCT
       Coffee bags. Menus. Packaging. Merch.
       Present, but never the lead.
```

Industry default: 80% product, 20% lifestyle.
**Estancia: the opposite.**

## 08 · Application Notes

### Sprint 4.1 — Volcanic Hospitality Visual Library (Day 1-2)
Prompts redistribute to 40/30/20/10. Architecture leads (~16 prompts). Ritual second (~12). Landscape third (~8). Product last (~4). Add `"in the style of Aman, Casona Sforza, Hotel 23 Medellín, Casa TO"` to anchor emotional territory.

### Figma Foundations (Day 3)
No change to tokens themselves. The system is sound. Variable naming may benefit from pillar tags (e.g., color-ritual, color-earth) — optional.

### Figma Frames (Day 4-5)
Consider reframing the 5 frames to lead with philosophy and architecture:

```
01 · HERO            Single architectural image + Cormorant tagline
02 · PHILOSOPHY      Four Pillars as visual essay (volcanic hospitality)
03 · THE PLACE       Architecture + landscape grid (40% + 20%)
04 · RITUAL          Coffee ceremony in volcanic setting (30%)
05 · STAY            Rooms + reservation, last (10% product folded in)
```

This puts STRATEGY first and BOOKING last — the inverse of most hospitality sites, and exactly the move that signals "hospitality philosophy" over "boutique hotel marketing."

### Figma Make (Day 6-7)
The prompt to Make becomes:
> "Convert these frames into a responsive hospitality philosophy site for Estancia Cafetera — a volcanic hospitality sanctuary in Tepoztlán. Preserve typography, spacing, and tokens exactly. Lead with architecture, support with ritual, anchor with landscape, accent with product. Restraint over decoration. Aman-tier finish. Mobile <768px: stack pillars vertically, collapse nav to hamburger."

### Build-in-Public (Day 8-9)
Lead content thesis:
> "I built a brand and discovered its philosophy in the process. Here's how AI helped me see what the brand was actually about — and how the 40/30/20/10 inversion turned a coffee project into a hospitality system."

## 09 · What's Out

To make this work, the following come off the moodboard and prompt library:

- Latte art close-ups
- Bistro chairs
- Decorative Talavera tile patterns (Talavera Blue stays on one object per room max)
- Kidney-tile pools
- Patterned cabinets
- Cup-and-bean editorial setups
- Smiling barista portraits
- "Coffee shop" interior tropes

Anything that reads as café branding rather than hospitality architecture is rejected.

---

## 10 · Glossary

**Volcanic Hospitality System** — Estancia's positioning category. Hospitality that draws material, emotional, and operational identity from volcanic landscape and slow ritual.

**40/30/20/10** — The visual library distribution ratio (Architecture · Ritual · Landscape · Product).

**Four Pillars** — Ritual · Earth · Hospitality · Craftsmanship. Every guest moment, design decision, or asset can be tested against these.

**Presence as product** — The thesis that what guests buy is not coffee, accommodation, or experience — but the state of being slowed down and reconnected.
