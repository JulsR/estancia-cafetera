01 — The Direction

Matter, not moment.

Every image, every video, every graphic must answer one question: does this feel observed, or does this feel produced? The brand only ships the first.

Light is a character

Single-source natural light. The hour before sunrise, the hour after. Long shadows. Specular highlights on stone, ceramic, copper. No flash, no fill, no HDR. If the room is dim, photograph the dimness.

Texture in the foreground

The closest thing to camera must show material truth — chisel marks on stone, throwing rings on ceramic, slubs on linen, crema on coffee. Never airbrushed, never staged.

People through hands

Show the work, not the face. Barista hands tamping. Farmer hands sorting cherries. Cook hands kneading. Guest hands holding a cup. Faces only in candid, observed moments — never to camera.

03 — Master Template

One structure. Forty prompts.

The locked Midjourney v7 prompt structure. Every shot in Section 02 fills these tokens. Never deviate from the camera, palette, or negative blocks — those are what keep the library consistent.

/* MASTER PROMPT — Midjourney v7 syntax */

[SUBJECT]      → filled per shot · see Section 04

[LIGHT]        morning side light from left, raking, long shadows, 7am,
                window light, soft falloff, single light source, no fill

[COMPOSITION]  off-center subject, generous negative space,
                rule-of-thirds, low camera angle, asymmetric framing

[MATERIAL]     hand-thrown stoneware, dark wood, copper patina,
                slubbed natural linen, volcanic stone, visible texture,
                lived-in, unpolished, hand-touched

[PALETTE]      warm terracotta and deep brown earth tones,
                ash white linen, copper accent, deep green foliage,
                muted color, no saturation, no neon, no bright primaries

[CAMERA]       medium format film photography, Mamiya 7,
                Kodak Portra 400, slight grain, shallow depth of field,
                f/2.8, soft focus falloff

[MOOD]         quiet, observed, intimate, candid documentary,
                no performance, no posing, morning silence

[NEGATIVE]     --no people facing camera, smiling, logos, signage,
                modern technology, plastic, neon, HDR, drone view,
                wide grin, stock photography, tourist

[FLAGS]        --ar 4:5 /* portrait · default · for web hero */
              --style raw /* less MJ stylization, more photographic */
              --stylize 200 /* low-to-mid styling, prevents over-rendering */
              --v 7
— Aspect ratios by use

--ar 4:5 for Framer hero portraits, social verticals. --ar 3:2 for Framer wide blocks, room galleries. --ar 16:9 for video stills, header bars. --ar 1:1 for Instagram grid, thumbnail tiles.

— Stylize discipline

--stylize 200 is the default. Going higher (400+) makes Midjourney add invented details — fake plants, ornamental serving ware, decorative kitsch. The brand fails. Never above 250.

07 — The Curation

Choosing what stays.

Midjourney will give you four options per prompt. You'll be tempted to pick the prettiest. Don't. Pick the most true — the one that looks observed, not produced.

— Keep when

  • The light direction matches the prompt — single source, side
  • The texture is visible at the foreground edge
  • Hands appear without faces — work made visible
  • The palette stays inside the warm earth family
  • The composition has real negative space
  • It looks like a moment caught, not a moment posed

— Kill when

  • The image has invented decorative objects — fake plants, ornamental serving ware
  • The lighting is symmetric — front fill, no shadow
  • A face is visible to camera, even in soft focus
  • The colors include neon, pure white, or saturated primaries
  • The composition is centered, brochure-like
  • It looks like a Pinterest mood board, not a hotel

The order matters.

Generate in this order to build a consistent library, not a scattered one. Each phase establishes the visual language the next phase inherits. Don't skip ahead.
