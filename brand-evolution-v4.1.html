01 — Why this evolution

What Tepoztlán actually is.

v4 imagined the brand. v4.1 was informed by standing there. The dark editorial register was right for the philosophy — but it didn't account for Mexican highland sun, Talavera blue tile, bougainvillea, or staff working the gardens at 5pm.

+ ADD Bright register · for property documentation, daytime, real photography
+ ADD Talavera Blue #2D4A7B · accent only · appears on craft objects (tile, ceramic)
+ ADD Bougainvillea #C04A6E · seasonal accent · appears as moments, not structure
+ ADD Real Photography Direction · companion to AI Image Direction
~ EVOLVE Image Direction Section · acknowledges both registers · context-aware
= KEEP Everything else · typography, voice, structure, dark base, philosophy
02 — The Two Registers

One brand. Two atmospheres.

The same restraint, expressed in two lights. Dark is for story. Bright is for place. The brand picks the right register for the surface — never mixes them within a single composition.

/ Register 01

Editorial Dark

For story, philosophy, the inside of the brand. Single-source light. Long shadows. Observed silence.

  • Single-source natural light, side or back
  • Long shadows, dim interiors photographed dim
  • Hand-thrown ceramic, dark wood, copper, candle
  • Terracotta and deep brown earth palette
  • No people, or hands without faces
  • The hour before sunrise, the hour after sunset
— Used forWebsite hero (editorial side)
Philosophy & story pages
Coffee program, mezcal cellar
Print collateral · menus
Section transitions
/ Register 02

Property Bright

For the place itself, in real Mexican sun. The hotel as it actually is — alive, occupied, full of pattern and bougainvillea.

  • Mid-morning to mid-afternoon natural light
  • Blue Mexican highland sky, bright shadows
  • Terracotta floor, white stucco arch, painted tile
  • Bougainvillea and foliage allowed as accents
  • Real staff working, no posing, no tourists
  • The Tepozteco visible when it can be — anchors place
— Used forProperty gallery · rooms
Restaurant · pool · grounds
Location & arrival pages
Social documentation
Press kit · brochures
03 — Accent Colors

Two new moments.

Neither is a system color. They never paint a button or a background. They appear in the world — on a tile, on a flower, on a sleeve of denim — and the brand allows them in without controlling them.

Talavera Blue

#2D4A7B · accent · craft only

The deep cobalt of Mexican Talavera tile. Appears on painted ceramic, hand-tiled walls, hand-painted plates. Centuries-old Mexican craft tradition.

— Use on Tile · ceramic · craft objects only
— Never on UI · buttons · text
— Never as background fill

Bougainvillea Magenta

#C04A6E · seasonal · nature only

The improbable magenta of bougainvillea cascading over stone walls. A color that should not work with the brand and does. Used as a moment, never a system.

— Use in Photography only · as it appears
— Never in graphic design or UI
— Never paired with Talavera Blue in same frame
04 — Real Photography Direction

When the camera is actually there.

Companion brief to the AI Image Direction. For when you, a hired photographer, or staff document the actual property. Same restraint principle — applied to real Mexican light.

— Keep when

  • The Tepozteco is in frame — it anchors the place
  • Terracotta floor tiles are visible in foreground
  • Bougainvillea appears organically — never staged
  • Staff are working, never posing, never to camera
  • Light is mid-morning or golden hour — bright, not flat
  • Empty tables, empty chairs — implies presence, not absence
  • Talavera tile patterns appear on real surfaces
  • Real cobblestone, real stone walls, real volcanic rock

— Kill when

  • Modern technology is visible — phones, laptops, screens
  • Power lines or traffic cones cut through the frame
  • Tourist props — sombreros, "Mexico" merchandise, kitsch
  • Posed faces, smiles to camera, group shots
  • Flat midday sun — wait for the light, always
  • Mannequins, signage, commercial intrusion
  • Pool umbrellas with stripes or branding
  • Anything that says "boutique hotel" before it says "Tepoztlán"

06 — Where AI fits now

The library has a second tier.

Real photography leads. AI fills the gaps — for what you can't photograph in a single trip, or won't have access to until the imagined property exists. Real is the source. AI is the supplement.

TIER 1 · Real photography
Used for Property documentation · place · staff · architecture · food
Source Your own camera or hired photographer
Register Mostly bright · occasionally dark

TIER 2 · AI imagery
Used for Coffee plant close-ups · dawn moments · abstract details
Source Midjourney v7 · the 40-prompt Sprint 4 library
