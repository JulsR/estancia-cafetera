Estancia Cafetera

A 100-year-old coffee estate in the volcanic highlands of Tepoztlán. Ten rooms. One restaurant. A working farm that became a hotel because the owners refused to let the land stop being used with intention.

01 — Philosophy

Volcanic restraint.

The brand doesn't announce itself. It reveals itself slowly — through the weight of the paper, the temperature of the palette, the precision of the type.

/ 01

Borrow from the land

Obsidian, ash, copper, deep green, terracotta. The palette comes from what the volcano left behind, not from a trend report.

/ 02

Borrow from the craft

Hand-stamped, imperfect, intentional. The seams show because they were meant to.

/ 03

Premium through patience

Not premium because of polish. Premium because nothing was rushed — including the silence between elements.

02 — The Guest

Three profiles. One brand.

Different motivations. Same standard. All three converge on what the brand refuses to be — performed at, themed up, or polished smooth.

/ Guest 01

The Coffee Pilgrim

"I came for the varietal. I stayed for the people who grow it."

Single-origin obsessive. Treats the estate as a destination, not an amenity. Knows the difference between washed and natural processing. Wants to meet the farmers, see the drying patios, understand this hillside's terroir.

— What they look for Varietal transparency
Farmer access
Tasting protocols
Process education
/ Guest 02

The Heritage Heir

"My grandparents had a hacienda. I'm looking for the real thing, not the costume."

Mexican or Mexican-American with ancestral ties to campo life. Speaks Spanish at home. Knows when something is authentic vs. themed. Pays for cultural fluency, refuses theatre.

— What they look for Real regional Spanish
No costume, no kitsch
Generational depth
Local sourcing visible
/ Guest 03

The Aesthete

"Texture over comfort. I'd rather feel something than be pampered."

Creative director, architect, brand founder. 32–48. Travels for material experience, not luxury benchmarks. Will pay $500/night without hesitation if the experience earns it.

— What they look for Intentional restraint
Material honesty
No performative luxury
Editorial-worthy moments
— Where all three converge —

None of these guests want to be performed at. All three reward quiet expertise, cultural fluency without theatre, and intentional restraint. The brand doesn't have to choose between them — it has to be true to all three simultaneously, which is the same thing as being true to the place itself.

05 — Voice

Specific, slow, understated.

The voice matches the typography: quiet, precise, literary — never decorative. We say what is true and stop talking.

— We say

"The coffee was planted in 1924. The rooms came later."

We don't

"Welcome to our luxurious historic boutique hotel experience!"

— We say

"Breakfast is at the time the kitchen is ready. Usually by seven."

We don't

"Enjoy our gourmet breakfast served daily from 7:00 AM to 10:00 AM."

— We say

"Ten rooms. No two are alike. None are accidental."

We don't

"Choose from our 10 beautifully designed unique accommodations!"

06 — Image Direction

Matter, not moment.

For commissioned photography and AI image briefs. One direction. Both surfaces. The image should feel observed, not produced.

01 · Volcanic Stone, Morning
02 · Coffee Plant, Low Light
03 · Mist Over Field
04 · Copper, Candlelit
05 · Dark Wood Interior
06 · Hand-thrown Ceramic
07 · Highland, Dawn
08 · Stone × Foliage
— Always
  • Natural light, single source
  • The hour before or after sunrise
  • Texture in the foreground
  • Negative space — let the mist do the work
  • Hands present, faces rarely
  • Medium-format film feel, slight grain
— Never
  • Wide grins, posed groups
  • HDR, lifted shadows, saturated skies
  • Drone shots without intention
  • Stock-style "lifestyle" framing
  • Logos, signage, modern technology visible
  • Fanned-out fruit on linen
07 — Surfaces

One system. Multiple surfaces.

What the tokens power. The proof this is a system, not a mood board.

SURFACE / 01

Framer Site

Public-facing. Editorial pacing. Type-led. Hero is the name, set in display. Tokens import directly as variables.

SURFACE / 02

Guest Portal

Pre-arrival and in-stay. Mono labels, display headings, restrained UI. The brand doesn't leave when you book.

SURFACE / 03

Print Collateral

Heavyweight stock. Letterpress where possible. Copper foil on the menu. Hand-stamped room numbers.

SURFACE / 04

AI Image Brief

Section 06 is the brief. Palette, light direction, subject hierarchy — all token-bound, all consistent.
